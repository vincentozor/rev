Nothing OS Sparks Outrage After Testing Lock-Screen Ads on Beta Users

October 28, 2025
PhoneSpecs Pro
Nothing OS
Lock Screen Ads
Android 16
Beta Testing
Phone 3a

Nothing, the smartphone company known for its minimalist approach and clean Android experience, is facing significant backlash from its user base after introducing lock screen advertisements in the Nothing OS 4.0 beta.

The controversial "Lock Glimpse" feature, discovered in the Android 16-based beta update for Nothing Phone 3a series devices, displays rotating wallpapers with embedded content links that function as advertisements for articles and promotions . The move represents a dramatic departure from Nothing's established brand identity of offering bloatware-free software.

Key Developments:

  • Lock Glimpse feature discovered in Nothing OS 4.0 beta for Phone 3a series
  • Feature displays rotating wallpapers with embedded advertisement links
  • Nothing confirms partnership with digital ad platform Bouyan
  • Feature is currently disabled by default but raises concerns about future direction
  • Company cites "razor-thin margins" as justification for exploring ad revenue

What is Lock Glimpse?

Lock Glimpse is a new feature introduced in Nothing OS 4.0 that displays automatically rotating wallpapers on the lock screen, with each image containing embedded links to external content . While described by Nothing as delivering "fresh, high-quality wallpapers" and "timely updates," the functionality closely resembles advertising platforms seen on budget devices from other manufacturers.

Rotating Wallpapers

Automatically cycles through curated images each time the display turns on

Embedded Links

Each wallpaper contains links to external articles and promotional content

Bouyan Partnership

Powered by Chinese digital ad platform similar to Glance lock screen service

Community Backlash and Response

The discovery of Lock Glimpse sparked immediate criticism across social media platforms and Nothing's community forums . Long-time supporters expressed feeling betrayed by what they perceive as a departure from the company's core principles of clean software and user experience.

One user on X (formerly Twitter) remarked, "Nothing did Something... adding ads with Lock Screen Glance (Glimpse), which sucks," while others lamented the potential erosion of the minimalist experience that attracted them to Nothing devices in the first place .

"We operate on razor-thin margins and must make deliberate choices to deliver a high-quality user experience while remaining financially sustainable."
- Akis Evangelidis, Nothing Co-Founder

Nothing's Official Response

In response to the growing controversy, Nothing co-founder Akis Evangelidis published a detailed explanation addressing community concerns . The company emphasized several key points in their defense:

Current Implementation

Nothing stresses that Lock Glimpse is currently disabled by default in the beta release and requires users to manually enable the feature . The company also highlighted that no personal data is collected when users choose to activate Lock Glimpse.

Business Justification

Evangelidis explained that as a relatively new smartphone manufacturer without diversified revenue streams, Nothing faces significant challenges with "razor-thin margins" and higher component costs compared to established players . The exploration of alternative revenue models, including Lock Glimpse and carefully selected third-party app partnerships, is presented as necessary for the company's financial sustainability.

Broader Industry Context

Nothing's move reflects broader trends in the smartphone industry, where manufacturers increasingly seek alternative revenue streams beyond hardware sales . Similar lock screen advertising platforms have proliferated, with services like Glance claiming over 450 million devices globally, primarily in emerging markets.

Other manufacturers have faced similar backlash for incorporating advertisements into their interfaces. Xiaomi previously scaled back system ads in MIUI following user pushback, while Samsung reduced placements in first-party apps after community feedback . The tension between maintaining clean software and exploring monetization opportunities represents an ongoing challenge across the industry.

How to Disable Lock Glimpse

For beta testers who encounter the Lock Glimpse feature, disabling it is straightforward :

  • Open Settings application on your Nothing device
  • Navigate to Lock screen > Lock Glimpse
  • Toggle off "Display on lock screen"
  • Additionally disable "Left swipe to explore" to prevent contextual content previews

These steps ensure no data transmission to advertising partners and restore the standard lock screen experience with user-selected wallpapers.

What This Means for Nothing's Future

The Lock Glimpse controversy represents a critical test for Nothing's relationship with its core user base . While the feature remains optional in the current beta, community concerns focus on whether this represents the beginning of a broader shift toward increased monetization of Nothing OS.

Nothing has indicated that future non-flagship devices may ship with carefully selected third-party applications pre-installed, though the company promises these will be easily removable and transparently communicated . How Nothing balances financial sustainability with its established brand identity will likely determine its ability to maintain the trust of the enthusiast community that has supported its growth.
